Prevention Point Pittsburgh (PPP) is a nonprofit organization based in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, that focuses on harm reduction and providing health services to individuals affected by substance use disorders and other related issues. Here are some of the key activities and services typically offered by Prevention Point Pittsburgh:

1.  ****Harm Reduction Services****: PPP operates a harm reduction program aimed at reducing the negative consequences associated with drug use. This includes providing sterile syringes and other injection equipment to prevent the transmission of bloodborne infections such as HIV and hepatitis C.

2.  ****Naloxone Distribution****: PPP distributes naloxone, a medication used to reverse opioid overdoses, to individuals at risk of experiencing or witnessing an overdose. Naloxone training and distribution programs are crucial in combating the opioid overdose crisis.

3.  ****HIV and Hepatitis C Testing and Prevention****: PPP offers testing for HIV and hepatitis C, as well as education and prevention services aimed at reducing the spread of these infections among people who inject drugs and other at-risk populations.

4.  ****Healthcare Services****: PPP provides basic healthcare services, such as wound care, screenings, and referrals to medical providers, to individuals who may not have access to traditional healthcare settings.

5.  ****Social Services and Support****: PPP offers case management and support services to help individuals access resources such as housing, food assistance, and substance use treatment.

6.  ****Community Outreach and Education****: PPP engages in community outreach and education efforts to raise awareness about harm reduction principles, substance use disorders, and related health issues. This may include participating in community events, providing educational materials, and conducting training sessions for healthcare professionals and community members.

Overall, Prevention Point Pittsburgh plays a vital role in promoting the health and well-being of individuals affected by substance use disorders in the Pittsburgh area through harm reduction initiatives, healthcare services, and community outreach efforts.
